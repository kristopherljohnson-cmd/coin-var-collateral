Value at Risk
VaR
Risk Measure
The maximum expected loss over a specified holding period at a given confidence level (95% or 99%), derived from historical log-return distributions. A 99% 3-day VaR of −15% means there is only a 1% chance of losing more than 15% over 3 days based on historical data.
Formula
VaR(α, h) = Percentile(R_h, α × 100)
R_h = h-day log returns. α = tail probability: 0.01 for 99% confidence, 0.05 for 95%. Left-tail picks the worst (lowest) percentile. Right-tail flips the sign first.
CVaR
Conditional VaR · Expected Shortfall
Risk Measure
The average of all losses that exceed the VaR threshold. Always more conservative than VaR — it answers “given that we breach VaR, what is the average loss?” Preferred by regulators (Basel III/IV) for capturing tail risk more completely than VaR alone.
Formula
CVaR(α) = E[R | R < VaR(α)]
Mean of all return observations strictly below the VaR threshold. If no observations fall below the threshold, CVaR = VaR.
Max Loss
Historical Worst Return
Risk Measure
The single worst h-day log return observed in the entire historical dataset. Unlike VaR and CVaR which are probabilistic, Max Loss is a deterministic worst-case drawn directly from history — capturing crashes, de-pegs, and liquidity crises.
Formula
MaxLoss(h) = min(R_h)
Right-tail mode: max(R_h). R_h is the full set of h-day log returns across all offset windows.
Tail Observations
Tail Breach Count
Risk Measure
The count of historical returns that breached the 99% VaR threshold. A high tail count relative to expected (1% × n) signals fat tails — the asset violates VaR more often than a normal distribution predicts. Useful for stress-testing model validity.
Formula
TailObs = count(R : R < VaR₉₉)
Under a true normal distribution, expected tail count ≈ 1% × n observations. Excess above this indicates fat tails.
Market Risk Premium
MRP
Risk Measure
The absolute value of the 99% N-day VaR. The pure price-risk component of the haircut — how much collateral value could be lost under a 99th-percentile adverse move over the holding period. The foundation upon which liquidity and additional haircuts are added.
Formula
MRP = |VaR₉₉(h)|
Always positive. Equals the magnitude of the worst expected loss at 99% confidence over the h-day holding period.
Log Return
Logarithmic Return
Statistics
The natural log of the price ratio between two periods. Preferred in risk models because they are time-additive, symmetrical, and better approximate a normal distribution than simple returns. h-day log returns are computed using multi-offset pooling for stability.
1-Day
r_t = ln(P_t / P_{t-1})
h-Day (multi-offset pooling)
R_h = ∪ₖ { ln(P_i / P_{i-h}) : i = k+h, k+2h, … }
All h offset windows are pooled to eliminate index-sensitivity and ensure return-count stability regardless of series length.
Ann. Volatility
Annualized Standard Deviation
Statistics
The standard deviation of 1-day log returns scaled to annual frequency. Higher volatility means wider return distributions, larger VaR estimates, and higher required haircuts. Crypto uses 365 (trades 24/7) vs equities which use 252 trading days.
Formula
σ_ann = σ(r₁) × √365
σ(r₁) = population standard deviation of all 1-day log returns in the lookback window.
Ann. Return
Annualized Mean Log Return
Statistics
The arithmetic mean of 1-day log returns scaled to annual frequency. Represents the average directional drift of the asset. A negative annualized return means the asset has trended downward on average over the lookback window.
Formula
μ_ann = mean(r₁) × 365
Scaled by 365 for crypto markets (continuous trading year-round).
Empirical Percentile
Historical Simulation Method
Statistics
VaR is computed by sorting observed returns and reading the relevant percentile — no parametric distribution assumed. This captures fat tails and asymmetry in crypto returns that normal distributions underestimate. Linear interpolation is used between adjacent sorted values.
Interpolation
P(p) = s[lo]×(1−frac) + s[hi]×frac
s = sorted returns. idx = (p/100)×(n−1). lo = ⌊idx⌋, hi = ⌈idx⌉, frac = idx − lo.
Haircut
Collateral Discount Rate
Collateral
The percentage reduction applied to collateral market value to arrive at its credit value. A 40% haircut on $100 of BTC means it is valued at only $60 as collateral. Protects the lender against adverse moves, illiquidity, and operational risk during the liquidation window. Capped at 99%.
Formula
Haircut = |VaR₉₉(h)| + LiqPrem + AddHC
Capped at 99% to avoid division-by-zero in collateral ratio. MRP = price risk | LiqPrem = liquidity risk | AddHC = user-defined discretionary cushion.
Collat. Ratio
Collateralization Ratio
Collateral
Collateral pledged divided by exposure value. A ratio of 1.5× means $150 of collateral for every $100 of exposure. Derived from the haircut and adjusted downward by the liquidity discount factor. Color-coded: green >1.6×, amber 1.3–1.6×, red <1.3×.
Formula
RawCollat = 1 / (1 − Haircut)CollatRatio = RawCollat × DiscountFactor
Discount Factor
Liquidity Quality Adjustment
Collateral
A multiplier applied to the raw collateral ratio. Score 10 = no discount (1.0). Score 0 = 50% discount (0.5). Each point below 10 reduces the factor by 0.05, penalizing illiquid or low-cap assets with a lower effective collateral ratio.
Formula
DiscountFactor = max(0.5, 1 − (10 − Score) × 0.05)
Score 10 → 1.00 | Score 7 → 0.85 | Score 4 → 0.70 | Score 0 → 0.50 (floored)
Additional Haircut
Add HC · Discretionary Buffer
Collateral
A user-defined flat percentage added on top of the VaR-derived haircut and liquidity premium. Captures model risk, concentration risk, regulatory buffers, or idiosyncratic counterparty risk not quantified by historical simulation. Default 10%.
No formula — direct user input added as a flat percentage to the total haircut.
Liquidity Premium
Liq. Prem.
Liquidity
A flat percentage added to the haircut to compensate for the risk that collateral cannot be sold at fair value within the holding period. Illiquid assets require larger price concessions during forced liquidation. Default 5%.
No formula — direct user input added as a flat percentage to the total haircut.
Volume Score
24h Trading Volume Score (0–5)
Liquidity
A 0–5 score based on 24-hour USD trading volume. Higher volume = greater market depth, tighter spreads, lower liquidation risk. Combined with Market Cap Score for the total Combined Score driving the discount factor.
Scoring Thresholds
≥$12.5B→5 | ≥$2.5B→4 | ≥$250M→3≥$50M→2 | ≥$5M→1 | <$5M→0
Sourced from Binance 24hr ticker (primary) or CoinGecko simple price (fallback).
Market Cap Score
Market Cap Score (0–5)
Liquidity
A 0–5 score based on total market capitalization. Larger cap = more institutional participation, greater liquidity, lower manipulation risk. Combined with Volume Score for the total Combined Score.
Scoring Thresholds
≥$500B→5 | ≥$100B→4 | ≥$10B→3≥$1B→2 | ≥$100M→1 | <$100M→0
Sourced from CoinGecko market_chart. Binance-only tokens receive MC = 0.
Combined Score
Total Liquidity Score (0–10)
Liquidity
Sum of Volume Score + Market Cap Score (0–10). Used as a filter gate (must meet minimum, default 7) and as the basis for the discount factor. Assets below the minimum score fail the liquidity eligibility test for collateral acceptance.
Formula
Score = VolumeScore + MarketCapScore
Score ≥ MinScore → Passes. Score < MinScore → Fails. Default MinScore = 7.
Volume Filter
Minimum 24h Volume Gate
Liquidity
A hard floor on 24-hour USD volume. Assets below this threshold automatically fail the eligibility gate regardless of their combined score. Default $1M. Synthetic data is exempt (no real volume available). Prevents ultra-illiquid tokens from being accepted as collateral.
Binary gate: Volume ≥ MinVol → passes | Volume < MinVol → fails. Synthetic data exempt.
CoinGecko
CG · Primary Price Source
Data Source
Primary source for historical daily prices and market cap via the /market_chart API. Built-in ID mapping for ~60 major coins. Requires ≥30 data points. Raises error on rate-limit (HTTP 429) or insufficient data. 10-second timeout per request.
Data source — no formula applicable.
Binance
BN · Secondary Failover
Data Source
Secondary source used when CoinGecko fails or is rate-limited. Fetches daily OHLCV klines using closing prices. Tries four Binance hosts in sequence. Market cap unavailable from Binance (MC scores as 0). Volume always sourced from Binance 24hr ticker regardless of which source provides price history.
Data source — no formula applicable.
Synthetic Data
SYN · Tertiary Fallback
Data Source
Used when both CoinGecko and Binance fail. Generates simulated price paths via Geometric Brownian Motion with per-ticker volatility parameters and a deterministic LCG seed (based on ticker name) for reproducibility. Assumes zero drift. Results carry a warning badge.
GBM Price Path
P_{t+1} = P_t × exp(μ·dt + σ·√dt · Z)
μ=0 (zero drift), dt=1/252, Z~N(0,1) via Box-Muller from LCG PRNG. Seed = Σ(charCode × 31^i) mod 2³².
Lookback Window
Historical Sample Period
Data Source
Calendar days of price history used to compute return distributions. Shorter windows (1 year) are more responsive to recent conditions but less stable. Longer windows are more stable but may underweight current volatility regimes. Basel III recommends ≥1 year for regulatory VaR.
User-selectable: 1 Year (365d), 2 Years (730d), or Full History (max available from source).
Holding Period
Liquidation Horizon (h days)
Model Parameter
Days assumed to fully liquidate the collateral in a stress scenario. VaR, CVaR, and Max Loss are all computed at this horizon. Longer holding period = greater price exposure = larger VaR. For illiquid assets, should reflect realistic liquidation timelines. Typical crypto: 1–3 days for majors.
Multi-offset pooling (h > 1)
R_h = ∪ₖ { ln(P_i/P_{i-h}) : i=k+h, k+2h,… }
Avoids the scaling approximation (σ_h ≈ σ_1×√h) by using actual h-day returns. All h offset windows pooled for stability.
Tail Direction
Left-Tail vs Right-Tail
Model Parameter
Controls whether VaR measures worst losses (left-tail, standard for collateral risk) or best gains (right-tail, for opportunity cost or structured products). Left-tail is the conventional choice for collateral haircut computation.
Implementation
Left: VaR = Percentile(R, 1%)Right: VaR = −Percentile(−R, 1%)
Right-tail flips sign before and after the percentile call, reusing the same left-tail logic.
Confidence Level
α · Significance Level
Model Parameter
Probability that the actual loss will not exceed the VaR estimate. Three levels computed: 95% (α=0.05), 99% (α=0.01), and 99.5% (α=0.005). The 99% level drives the haircut. Higher confidence = larger VaR = more conservative haircut.
Levels computed
95% : Percentile(R, 5th)99% : Percentile(R, 1st) ← haircut basis99.5%: Percentile(R, 0.5th)
Basel III IMA standard uses 99.5% for market risk capital; 99% is the collateral convention here.
Auto-Failover
Data Source Priority Chain
Model Parameter
The engine tries data sources in fixed priority order: CoinGecko first (best market cap + history), then Binance (best for major pairs), then Synthetic (always available). Every token always produces a result. Volume is always sourced from Binance 24hr ticker first regardless of which source provides prices.
Priority Chain
1. CoinGecko /market_chart (10s timeout)2. Binance klines × 4 hosts (6s each)3. Synthetic GBM (always succeeds)
Volume: Binance 24hr ticker × 4 hosts → CoinGecko simple → 0. Synthetic is exempt from the volume filter gate.
